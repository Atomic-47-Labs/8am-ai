## 8am AI!

### Summary

David Olsson, Fulvio Ciano, Jen Boger, Ying, Hanif Janmohamed, and Juan Rojas discussed the landscape of agentic AI, comparing paradigms such as the overarching system (Claudoc), integrated chatbots, and personal assistants (Granola), noting that the current state of tools, despite significant improvement, still presents challenges in integration, context continuity, and memory. Participants expressed frustration with the need for constant supervision, the fragmented nature of chat interfaces, and the AI's struggle to maintain memory and offer plurality of thought, leading to discussions on the importance of structured memory, intuitive interfaces beyond chat, and robust critical prompts to guide AI outcomes. The group concluded that while the tools are currently powerful for exploratory work, the necessary "protocols" for effective practice are not yet fully integrated into the user experience.

### Details

- **Discussion on Agentic AI and Interfaces (Claudoc, Granola, and Chatbot Paradigms)**: David Olsson identified three interesting paradigms in the new "agentic space". One paradigm, exemplified by Claudoc, acts as a "human sitting over top" of the system. Another paradigm is the integrated chatbot.
- **Chatbot Structure and Tooling (Agents, Artifacts, Skills)**: David Olsson detailed the internal structure of their demonstrated chatbot, explaining that it relies on the LLM as a system to determine which tools to use, with underlying MCPs and agents.
- **Challenges with Tooling and Workflow Generation**: David Olsson demonstrated attempting to use an artifact they built to chat workflows. They faced a challenge where the system struggled to call the correct artifact.
- **Alternative AI Agentic Systems (Personal Assistants)**: David Olsson outlined a third paradigm: personal assistant AI agentic systems like Granola, which allow traversing a knowledge worker's entire ecosystem.
- **Individual Approaches to AI Practice and Modalities**: Fulvio Ciano responded by differentiating between the model and the interface to the model. Fulvio Ciano asserted that Google has a major advantage because it is the "substrate for just a lot of work".
- **Frustrations with AI Utility and Interface Gaps**: Jen Boger affirmed that AI is "so useful but not useful enough yet," comparing it to managing a "pre-teen" that needs constant supervision.
- **Contrasting Views on Integration and Magnetic Attractors**: David Olsson reflected on the integration patterns, contrasting Google's approach with David's preferred model, where the chat environment is primary.
- **The Future of Interfaces and Multimodality**: Ying argued that current interfaces are "inside the box" and not the future, proposing that the future should involve intuitive and "lovable" interfaces.
- **The Role of Conversation, Continuity, and Granularity**: Hanif Janmohamed expressed enjoyment in "collaborative engagement" with AI as an extension of their own thinking. Hanif Janmohamed's "pet peeve" with chat is its fragmented nature.
- **Challenges with Context and Memory in Code Work**: Juan Rojas described a painful experience using Copilot in Visual Studio where the tool lost context after they committed their work.
- **The Role of Memory and Skills in AI Experience**: Juan Rojas found the memory feature in ChatGPT interesting, as it guides answers but also limits them. Juan Rojas affirmed that skills will be important, potentially evolving into distinct "personalities".
- **The Distinction Between Context and Memory**: Fulvio Ciano suggested practical strategies for dealing with continuity loss in coding, such as using a "memory bank" workflow.
- **Challenges of Cognitive Constraint and Plurality in AI**: Jen Boger observed that AI tends to shove the user into a specific line of thinking, which is hard to escape.
- **Frustrations and Positive Outlooks on Current AI Tools**: David Olsson summarized that despite the issues, the tools are significantly better than they were three years ago.
- **Potential of strong critical prompts for AI output**: David Olsson shared a perspective that a very strong prompt can be created that will provide deep criticism as a road map out of a problem.
