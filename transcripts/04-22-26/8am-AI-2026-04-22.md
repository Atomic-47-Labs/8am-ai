### Summary

Meeting explored AI-driven systemic shifts, knowledge orchestration for development efficiency, and pedagogical challenges regarding foundational student learning.

**Systemic Shifts and Orchestration**
Participants identified a transition from design thinking to system thinking, emphasizing high-level orchestration over manual coding. Advanced models now act as autonomous agents that harvest signals to drive development.

**Knowledge Structures and Mastery**
Centralizing understanding through wiki-style corpora or markdown files enables more effective AI interactions. Mastery remains essential, as engineering backgrounds are critical for architectural discipline and high-quality outputs.

**Educational Paradigms and Complexity**
Educators highlighted a critical struggle with student reliance on AI tools at the expense of foundational knowledge. The group decided to increase problem complexity to force deeper learning.

### Details

* **Speaker Introduction and Meeting Context**: Mike Grell introduced themself, sharing their background that includes programming on a Commodore 64 and working at IBM for 30 years, and they are now focused on using AI for various development tasks. The group meets weekly to discuss the impact of AI on their work, and the current discussion centers on the "crazy nature" of how AI is evolving.

* **Impact of Advanced AI Models**: David Olsson shared their experience with a recent Anthropic model, describing it as a "significant thing" with an ecosystem that quickly facilitates useful business and programming outcomes. They noted that current frontier models offer a "fairly significant" learning loop and powerful capability for long-form workflows and reasoning, suggesting this represents a "tipping point".

* **Academic Challenges and AI in Course Design**: Ying mentioned their involvement in the BC campus AI network board and plans to lead a discussion in June about course redesign and assessment because the current model of assigning homework that students offload to AI is ineffective for learning. Ying stated they want students to use AI but are seeking input on what students need to learn beyond basic AI knowledge and tool use, with a key challenge being getting students engaged in learning and attending class.

* **Shift to System Thinking and Orchestration**: David Olsson discussed the shift from design thinking, which used to be valuable for problem-solving, toward "system thinking," which is now increasingly systematic and requires operating at a higher level closer to objectives and goals. They explained that their current work involves focusing on orchestration rather than code or low-level skills, which allows for the management of different control planes.

* **The Centrality of Understanding in AI Use**: Fulvio Ciano asserted that understanding the concept of "understanding" is now central in AI, referencing YouTuber Kurt J. Mongle, who emphasizes that AI can perform tasks, fetch information, and synthesize data, but it cannot give the user understanding. They concluded that coders must gain understanding of the project and what AI can do for them to use it effectively.

* **Feedback Loop Between User and AI Understanding**: Mike Grell agreed with the importance of understanding, noting that users must understand what they are trying to accomplish to effectively use AI, creating a "feedback loop" where the user gives the AI understanding, and in turn, the AI can enhance the user's understanding. Fulvio Ciano further noted that if a user provides vague instructions, the AI's failure to execute often exposes the user's lack of understanding of what they wanted, as insufficient detail was provided.

* **Developing and Structuring Contextual Knowledge**: Fulvio Ciano discussed the need for a "corpus of information" that is developed from user understanding and is critical for the AI to perform its job effectively. There is ongoing experimentation regarding the best way to structure this information—including vector databases, documents, and MD files—with a current strong consideration for using a wiki to structure data in a way that both users and the AI can easily derive understanding.

* **The Wiki Approach for Understanding and Maintenance**: Fulvio Ciano elaborated that using a wiki is not about finding the most efficient data storage method but about having a structure that enables the collection, storage, presentation, and critical "upkeep" of understanding, which addresses the challenges of maintaining consistency across multiple documents. David Olsson stated that they built a version of this pattern, called "app state," using markdown files instead of a wiki, which functions similarly by providing a corpus of work and making it structural.

* **AI as a Collaborative Partner and Signal Harvesting**: Hanif Janmohamed noted that working with AI feels like a "constantly evolving paradigm" and that they are impressed by how they can leverage their intuition and get feedback from their AI collaborator. David Olsson described a method called "signal harvesting," where a series of AI skills examines conversations and documents across various platforms (Slack, Notion, Gmail, repos) to find "the glue" and "synergies" that provide collective feedback and expose structure in the communication.

* **Autonomous AI Actors and Workflow Triggers**: David Olsson explained that the "app state" evolved to become an actor on its own, reporting to the users and harvesting data. The system now performs background work based on mechanical triggers like commits to Git, receiving an email, or cron jobs, which allows it to do work and surface patterns that would otherwise consume a significant amount of the user's time and capacity.

* **Increased Efficiency and the Need for Mastery**: Juan reported that adding more context and guardrails to the system improves the quality of AI-generated answers, and they are restructuring their team because developers are still needed for complex tasks. They built a personalized app in one week that equated to the work of four developers for a year and a half, dramatically increasing their code production. However, Juan still believes that mastery is needed, emphasizing that their engineering background has been vital for handling architectural and system-level disciplines.

* **Maintenance Paradigm Shift to AI Management**: Hanif Janmohamed questioned the paradigm for code maintenance, suggesting that it might be deferred to the AI, to which David Olsson agreed, stating that the AI is the best at eating its own dog food. David Olsson explained that the app is now designed to be driven by a sophisticated system of skills and markdown files, allowing the LLM to provide the intelligence for decision-making regarding what should be built next.

* **Educational Struggle with Foundational Knowledge**: Ying shared their struggle with current university students (ages 18-22) who use AI to generate outcomes and possess a "false confidence" that they know everything, but they lack the foundational knowledge and the capacity to assess the quality of the AI-generated output. Ying is grappling with how to bring back learning, knowledge acquisition, and basic foundations, as the established educational structure is no longer effective in this new environment.

* **Future Collaboration Tools and Increased Problem Complexity**: The group briefly discussed the potential value of a shared chat group and a "shared chatbot" with the same ongoing session in memory. Juan suggested that if AI makes simple work easy for students, the solution in education is to increase the complexity of the problems they must solve, forcing them to use AI to find a learning framework and build knowledge.
