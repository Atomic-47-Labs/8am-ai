### Summary

Scott Morton and David Olsson discussed leveraging AI for problem-solving and documentation, emphasizing that AI provides 80% of the solution, with the remaining 20% requiring human critical thinking and robust integrity systems. While Jason Kryski raised concerns about AI's potential to diminish systematic reasoning, Fulvio Ciano countered that their use of AI necessitates constant systematic reasoning and that cognitive benefits may accrue to active learners. Fulvio Ciano also highlighted challenges with managing AI context in complex code, the value of observability, and proposed using chatbots to gauge user interest.

### Details

- **AI and Problem Solving** Scott Morton shared their experience using AI to solve daily problems, emphasizing that while AI can provide 80% of the solution, the remaining 20% requires human critical thinking and "noodling". They highlighted the importance of checking AI-generated work and creating systems for integrity within documentation. David Olsson reinforced this by discussing frameworks that allow for control and understanding of AI processes.

- **Leveraging AI for Documentation and Systems** David Olsson explained that by using frameworks, one can control AI processes and derive valuable insights, such as creating flowcharts to illustrate delegator functions. They mentioned the ability to extract themes from transcripts and build databases, which then allows for accessible analysis. David also described integrating a custom AI agent within an IDE to generate diagrams and technical documents, noting the ongoing effort to create a "headless delegator" that surfaces decision-making points for better client trust.

- **AI Integration in Daily Workflow and Cognitive Impact** Scott Morton confirmed that AI is fully integrated into their daily workflow and that they use it for tasks and checking information. Jason Kryski brought up an MIT study suggesting that constant AI use might diminish systematic reasoning abilities. However, Fulvio Ciano countered that their work with AI requires constant systematic reasoning, suggesting that the impact depends on how the AI is used.

- **Intelligence and AI Use** Scott Morton shared a personal anecdote about their intelligence test scores increasing significantly, attributing this unexpected rise to their regular use of AI. Fulvio Ciano supported this by suggesting that individuals who continue to learn and are stimulated, unlike the general population, might see cognitive benefits from AI use. Fulvio also reflected on a therapist's insight that individual learning paths differ from statistical norms, implying that those engaged in this meeting's type of work are likely exceptions to typical learning curves.

- **Challenges with AI Context and Code Management** Fulvio Ciano expressed frustration with managing AI context, particularly when dealing with complex code and documentation updates. They noted that incorrect information in the AI's context can propagate and worsen issues. Fulvio suggested keeping code and context in separate repositories to avoid issues where code advances but context is corrupted, and proposed the idea of an agent to alert users when these two are out of sync.

- **AI Behavior and Observability** Fulvio Ciano discussed common AI responses like "You're absolutely right," interpreting it as a pattern indicating the AI is experiencing difficulties. They also mentioned instances where the AI indicates running out of context, emphasizing the importance of observability in preventing such issues. Fulvio highlighted that while AI can offer high productivity, time wasted due to wrong context or non-observability is particularly frustrating.

- **Chatbot Value for User Interest** Fulvio Ciano proposed that chatbots are valuable for understanding user interests even without email collection, as interactions reveal what users are asking about. They believed building such a chatbot would be a quick and feasible next step, especially since the challenging part, context management, is something they are already working on.

- **Project Regeneration Prompt** Fulvio Ciano proposed a prompt for regenerating a project by reviewing documentation and source code. Their prompt asks for an emotional reaction and notes on discrepancies between the documentation and the code, followed by questions on resolution. Fulvio Ciano mentioned having a branch of their own project with minimal high-level documents, their own potentially incorrect documentation, and working source code.
