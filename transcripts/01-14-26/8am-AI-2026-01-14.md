### Summary

David Olsson welcomed participants including Fulvio Ciano, Hanif Janmohamed, Juan Rojas, Jen Boger, and Jason Kryski, and introduced the Stone Maps project, which Hanif Janmohamed described as NFC-enabled "talking pet rocks" connected to a PWA with an AI-driven "planetary emissary" designed to encourage relational conversation and facilitate deeper human connections. ### Details

- **Introduction and Welcome** David Olsson opened the meeting and shared a recollection of making haikus with Fulvio Ciano about a year prior.
- **Introduction to the Stone Maps Project** Hanif Janmohamed described the project as a "stone map" with an NFC tag on the back that connects to a phone and opens a progressive web app (PWA).
- **Stone Map Features and Vision** Hanif Janmohamed explained that the project has been distilled down to a "talking pet rock with an AI component" that includes a conversational piece. The project includes a constitutional framework for the chatbot, called a "planetary emissary," designed to encourage relational conversation.
- **Goal and Request for Pilot Program** Hanif Janmohamed stated the project's goal is to facilitate deeper human-to-human connection through relational conversations. The immediate ask is for help to stand up the chatbot and finish the PWA.
- **Facilitating Relationships and Stone Agency** Hanif Janmohamed explained that a future state involves stones that can "understand proximity," allowing them to suggest and encourage useful connections.
- **University Partnership and Research Potential** Jen Boger suggested connecting with universities, specifically mentioning the University of British Columbia Okanagan (UBCO) and Emily Carr University.
- **Monetization through Provenance and Connection** Fulvio Ciano suggested the stones have the potential to monetize by leveraging their provenance and history of participation in events.
- **Commercial Focus and Ambient AI** David Olsson supported Fulvio Ciano's point, advising Hanif Janmohamed to prioritize commercialization and market entry.
- **Stone Identity and Event-Based Release** Fulvio Ciano suggested tying the stone's release to a high-value event, like Woodstock or Burning Man.
- **Handling Loss, Copying, and Pairing** Juan Rojas praised the idea and asked if multimodality would be incorporated. Hanif Janmohamed confirmed that the PWA already implements a media library for uploading various media.
- **Addressing Creep Factor and Equitable Access** Jen Boger raised concerns about the "creep factor" related to the intimacy of the stone and device interaction.
- **Age Well Connection and User Testing** Jen Boger suggested connecting Hanif Janmohamed with Age Well, a group focused on technology for supporting aging.
- **Focusing on Practical Steps and Simplicity** David Olsson urged Hanif Janmohamed to break into the market instead of doing more research.
- **User Engagement and Market Niche** Jason Kryski suggested that the "Redditor archetype" might be the right niche to target initially.
- **Rock Project Virality and Engagement** Jason Kryski and Hanif Janmohamed discussed the idea of rocks as a paperweight or decorative item.
- **Next Milestones and Stone Project Commercial Scope** Hanif Janmohamed identified the next milestone for the project as gaining traction on prototypes, standing up a back end, and potentially hosting "studio sessions".
- **Personal Changes and Relocation** Hanif Janmohamed shared that they are currently in central Mexico. Their son recently accepted a job in Budapest, Hungary.
- **Project Collaboration and Future Steps** Hanif Janmohamed reiterated their desire to move the stones project forward.
