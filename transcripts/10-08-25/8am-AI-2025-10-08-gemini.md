## 8am AI!

### Summary

David Olsson demonstrated Fireflies for transcript summaries and an Agentic AI for organizational simulations, using 800 transcripts as training data, which accurately predicted challenges in early versions. Travis Worthing inquired about the simulation process, while Spencer MacBeth and Fulvio Ciano discussed data integration challenges with LLMs. Ying suggested focusing on consumer pain points, and David Olsson responded that the demo was for market opportunities, shifting their sales approach to AI as a process rather than a product.

### Details

- **Leveraging Fireflies for Transcript Summaries** David Olsson demonstrated how Fireflies, integrated with their MCP Firefly account, can generate summaries from meeting transcripts. This system processes around 800 transcripts to provide strategic insights and simulate organizational changes, leveraging historical collaboration patterns and individual working styles.

- **Agentic AI for Organizational Simulation** David Olsson presented a capability for Agentic AI to simulate changes in team structures, leadership, and organizational design using 800 transcripts as training data. This simulation tool aims to provide foresight into collaboration, performance, and engagement outcomes, and can generate reports on predicted results.

- **Practical Implementation of Agent Simulations** David Olsson discussed the practical implementation roadmap for building agent simulations, emphasizing the use of historical meeting data to create personas and run simulations. They also noted that the LLM is capable of measuring and evaluating conversations to derive statistical values for analysis.

- **Agent Workflow and Specifications** David Olsson explained that the system can build a specification from a conversation, which then runs inside their IDE. This workflow allows for the creation of rigorous specifications that are then distilled into an MVP plan, and the system can build it based on a requested text stack.

- **Statistical Simulation Analysis** Travis Worthing inquired about the simulation process, and David Olsson clarified that it involves creating agents, processing conversations, and deriving a statistical understanding of participants, including their connections, conversation types, and decision-making styles. This analysis is then applied to scenarios, which were derived from events within Atomic 47 and have changed organizational structures.

- **Challenges and Accuracy of Simulations** David Olsson shared that early versions of the simulation, focusing on 100 meetings with fewer participants, accurately predicted them to be overloaded and in need of assistance with work reallocation. Jen Boger affirmed the accuracy of this prediction.

- **Agent-based Workflows and Cloud Code Templates** David Olsson detailed how different MCPs function within the LLM framework, supporting workflows and ideation. They mentioned the use of Cloud Code Templates, a project that allows agents to be placed in directories and markdown files, providing hooks for MCPs and supporting both command-line interface and UI.

- **WorkSona MCP Server for Local Use** David Olsson plans to share their WorkSona MCP server, which is a local Node package allowing users to utilize a local MCP server. This system provides standardized agents and reporting, facilitating workflows and allowing users to reference agents for tasks like allocating team members for project planning.

- **Delegator System and Agent Interoperability** David Olsson explained that the delegator system acts as its own MCP and determines which agents to activate. They noted that agents can create workflows, which are typically linear but can also support parallel work, and function at the command line level.

- **Observability and Cost Considerations in Cloud Code** David Olsson highlighted the move towards Cloud Code Templates for better observability of agent usage and effectiveness. They mentioned that some tasks in Cloud Code can be significantly expensive and emphasized the importance of understanding and managing these costs.

- **Agent Integration and Barriers to Adoption** David Olsson showcased 18 teams of agents developed for research within Cloud Code. They acknowledged that setting up these agents can be a barrier due to complex installation processes and configuration differences across operating systems.

- **LLM Capabilities in Orchestration and Delegation** David Olsson emphasized that LLMs possess inherent orchestration and delegation capabilities. They noted that while LLMs can effectively build applications, their reasoning capabilities are best utilized for tasks like analysis and knowledge work, despite some challenges with hallucination after about 75% of a task completion.

- **Data Integration Challenges with LLMs** Spencer MacBeth and Fulvio Ciano discussed the difficulties of natively integrating LLMs with data sources like Google Suite and AWS. Fulvio Ciano particularly noted Google's lack of native folder attachment for AI Studio and Gemini, which they believe is a crucial missing piece for full integration.

- **Value of Historical Data and AI's Role in Business** David Olsson underlined the value of having a dataset of 800 meetings, enabling the use of LLMs for assertion and inference grounded against historical norms. They also discussed how businesses are struggling with rapid organizational change and see LLMs as a tool to help understand the impact of such changes on people.

- **News and Perception of AI** David Olsson expressed frustration with the news's perception of AI, contrasting it with the practical experience of developing and deploying AI solutions. They noted that the traditional methods of addressing concerns about new technologies, such as cost, are no longer holding true due to the rapid pace of change in AI.

- **Scaling Challenges with Fireflies MCP** David Olsson acknowledged that their Fireflies MCP is not scaling effectively for their team, necessitating a move to another solution. They have built an application to download the 3,200 files associated with the 800 transcripts.

- **Communication and Sharing in the Team** David Olsson emphasized the need for better internal communication and sharing of their work, suggesting a Slack channel as a more rapid sharing platform than weekly meetings. Myles Holt offered to follow up with Jason regarding the Slack channel.

- **Addressing Consumer Pain Points with AI Solutions** Ying provided feedback on the demo, suggesting that David Olsson's team should focus on articulating the specific pain points their solutions solve for consumers. They emphasized the importance of presenting practical, simple examples that leaders can easily communicate to their employees to gain buy-in.

- **Strategic Positioning of AI Research** David Olsson responded that the demo showcased experiments to identify market opportunities rather than direct product solutions for current problems. They also mentioned that the discovered scenarios based on their organization's 100 meetings demonstrate the potential for AI to model organizational design and leadership challenges.

- **Product vs. Process in Sales** David Olsson explained that their approach to sales has shifted from selling AI as a product to selling it as a process. They believe this allows them to adapt to the rapidly changing AI landscape and avoid being undercut by new developments in the market.

- **Staying Engaged with AI Development** David Olsson stressed the importance of staying deeply engaged with AI developments to address the social aspects of its adoption and provide leadership in its use. They acknowledged the challenge of navigating the public's perception of AI, largely shaped by news, while focusing on practical applications and organizational upskilling.
