## 8am AI!

### Summary

Participants, including Jason Kryski, Fulvio Ciano, and David Olsson, discussed the significant disruption and uncertain future of work due to the rapid advancement of AI, agreeing that the ability to plan long-term is highly challenging and that individual competency with AI tools and active strategy is essential. David Olsson detailed their company's "takeoff tool" that uses large language models for OCR and processing structured data from unstructured documents, while Jason Kryski and Fulvio Ciano explored the difficulties of extracting domain expertise from individuals and suggested that AI needs to develop adaptive, growing context rather than relying on static interviews. Key talking points included the speed of AI adoption in the enterprise sector, the "recursive self-learning automation" concept, the shift in business models toward value creation, and the importance of an "inversion strategy" using critical prompts to analyze client problems and operationalize solutions.

### Details

- **Discussion on Health and Sickness**: The participants opened the meeting by discussing their ongoing illnesses.
- **Introduction to the Takeoff Tool and OCR Strategy**: David Olsson presented their "takeoff tool" used in oil and gas for generating a bill of material from drawings. They rely on LLMs from OpenAI for Optical Character Recognition and processing.
- **AI Functionality and Processing Time**: Processing takes 10 to 15 minutes for 60 pages due to the large number of tokens required, but this is faster than manual takeoff.
- **Future of Work and Recursive Automation**: Jason Kryski raised the question of what work will look like in three to eighteen months. Jason Kryski introduced the concept of "recursive self-learning automation," referencing a Web4 Conway pattern.
- **Open Claw and Evolutionary Learning Loops**: David Olsson discussed the "Open Claw" concept, which introduces an evolutionary learning loop. Jason Kryski agreed that current LLMs are capable enough to perform almost any task.
- **Uncertainty of Work in the Next 12 Months**: Jason Kryski expressed difficulty imagining what their work will be like in 12 months, as the application and distribution of AI technology are highly uneven across companies.
- **Inability to Plan for the Long Term**: Fulvio Ciano expressed frustration with trying to answer questions about the 6 to 18-month future, arguing that it is nearly impossible and that current problems should drive the application of current tools.
- **The "George Jetson Button" Scenario**: Jason Kryski contemplated the possibility that all current learned skills could become pointless within 18 months. Fulvio Ciano maintained that learning and applying skills to current problems holds value.
- **Value of Current Effort and Transition**: David Olsson framed this disruption as "welcome to the coal mine," emphasizing that for the individual, the transition can happen tragically fast.
- **AI Orchestration and Value Extraction**: Fulvio Ciano suggested that the future involves becoming "AI orchestrators" who oversee the AI.
- **Existential Crisis and Societal Impact**: Jason Kryski highlighted the rapid adoption of AI in the enterprise sector, suggesting a potential "dystopian fracturing of society," where job displacement leads to social unrest, resulting in universal basic income (UBI).
- **Future Strategy for Individuals and Organizations**: David Olsson agreed that the societal divide will grow and that individuals must actively figure out their future role.
- **Personal Strategy and Value Chain**: David Olsson shared that their current programming work is a side project to explore potential future value.
- **Business Models and Value Creation**: Jason Kryski discussed a business model shift where software-as-a-service (SaaS) charges are collapsing in favor of pricing based on value creation.
- **SMB and Local Business Application**: Jason Kryski outlined an initiative with Bailey to apply AI to small and medium businesses (SMBs) and local companies.
- **Domain Expertise Extraction**: Jason Kryski asked for opinions on efficiently extracting domain expertise, noting that much of their own knowledge is unconscious and has become "unknown unknowns".
- **Developing Adaptive Context**: Fulvio Ciano recognized the need to collect core context and experience but noted that the current method of extracting this knowledge via static questions is inefficient.
- **Inversion and Critical Prompts for Analysis**: David Olsson advised using a series of critical prompts to analyze isolated, unresolved situations to find intelligence, describing it as an inversion strategy.
- **The Foundational Question of Desire**: Jason Kryski and Fulvio Ciano concurred that the most critical and often ill-defined hurdle with clients is answering the question, "What do you want?".
- **Trust and Mitigating Risk**: David Olsson discussed the notion of trust and getting at the "why" behind client motivation.
- **The Inversion of Evaluation**: David Olsson concluded by emphasizing the power of the inversion principle, where the service provider evaluates the potential client rather than the other way around.
