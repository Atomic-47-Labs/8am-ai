Dec 3, 2025

## 8am AI!

### Summary

David Olsson reported on the sentiment that the transformer approach to AI is plateauing, which has led to calls for a return to fundamental research and discussion of the current AI technology as a potential "bubble." The participants, including Ying, David Olsson, and Hanif Janmohamed, discussed systemic issues like corporate tax avoidance and the potential future necessity of Universal Basic Income (UBI) due to AI-driven job displacement, which Fulvio Ciano and Hanif Janmohamed argued would require major systemic change to implement. Fulvio Ciano provided historical analogies for AI's disruptive potential, while Jason Kryski expressed caution about widespread income support and shared information about the use of LLMs to predict human behaviors and the "Build Canada" initiative for localized AI enablement; Ying also expressed interest in this initiative for community benefit.

### Details

- **AI Plateauing and the Call for Research** David Olsson reported on the current industry conversation, which suggests the transformer approach to AI is plateauing and will not achieve Artificial General Intelligence (AGI). They indicated that the thought leaders believe the incremental gains will diminish and become more costly, leading some early researchers, like Ilya, to call for a return to fundamental research. David Olsson noted that the investment in data centers to power these large language models (LLMs) represents a brute force approach, which is not sustainable long-term.

- **The AI Technology Bubble and Market Cap** David Olsson mentioned the sentiment that the current technological inflection point for AI could be considered a "bubble," as awareness shifts beyond the initial novelty. They stated that OpenAI has leveraged its partners into massive debt to expand data center capabilities, a brute-force effort to increase intelligence. David Olsson highlighted the unprecedented nature of the fast technology curve but suggested that the day-to-day experience at work might not change significantly, though innovation speed might decrease.

- **Tax Avoidance and Wealth Distribution** Ying expressed concern about large corporations avoiding real taxes by utilizing tax havens. David Olsson countered that in capitalism, politics often follows the lead of capital, making it unlikely for the government to successfully target tech companies for more taxes. Hanif Janmohamed pointed out that the situation is complicated because corporations were granted the rights of an individual, allowing companies like Apple to legally hold large, tax-free cash reserves.

- **Universal Basic Income (UBI) and Social Change** Ying expressed hope for the eventual implementation of universal basic income (UBI) as AI is expected to eliminate many jobs. David Olsson projected that UBI is likely to be introduced because it will be more cost-effective than existing programs like unemployment insurance. David Olsson cited the guaranteed income provided to their brother due to health issues as an existing example of a subsistence income.

- **Sovereignty over Natural Resources and Wealth Distribution** Ying raised the issue of natural resources, specifically oil and gas in Canada, being exploited for the profit of a few, rather than benefiting the general population as a commonwealth. Hanif Janmohamed suggested this Scandinavian model of a common fund from natural resources as a relevant comparison. David Olsson and Hanif Janmohamed emphasized that major systemic change, such as a revolution or catastrophic collapse, would be required to shift the business model away from the profit-driven approach of corporations holding vast wealth.

- **Historical Analogies for Societal Disruption** Fulvio Ciano provided historical analogies for the potential disruptive impact of AI, comparing it to the Black Plague and the Industrial Revolution. Fulvio Ciano noted that the Black Plague significantly reduced the labor supply, which altered the value of labor and led to the Renaissance. Fulvio Ciano suggested that AI could cause a disruption far exceeding these events by fundamentally changing the amount and value of labor.

- **AI's Impact on Employment and Policy Change** Ying asked if the disruption from AI replacing entry-level jobs would be significant enough to cause a bottom-up challenge to current policies on wealth distribution. Jason Kryski expressed caution about implementing policies like widespread income support, suggesting that such interventions could negatively alter the incentive structure of human beings. Ying argued that Scandinavian countries, which operate with a socialist and commonwealth model, have happier populations and that a positive environment can be contagious, promoting community well-being.

- **Simulation and Modeling of Human Behavior** Jason Kryski shared a link to a methodology using LLMs to predict human behaviors with high accuracy, suggesting it could be used to simulate different social scenarios. David Olsson supported the idea of simulation backed by data, noting that LLMs are built on human conversations and markers of behavior.

- **Complexity of Scandinavian Social Models** David Olsson complicated the view of Scandinavia by noting that while they have strong civic culture and welfare systems, countries like Sweden have high rates of youth alcoholism. Jason Kryski added that these societies are fragile, pointing to the difficulties they have experienced integrating immigration.

- **Influence and Engagement with Business Drivers** David Olsson, reflecting on their own experience as an entrepreneur, argued that influence requires engaging with the people who drive business decisions, even those focused solely on the bottom line. David Olsson emphasized the tension between doing good and participating in systems that might require small compromises. Hanif Janmohamed noted that human nature, which is inherently subject to context, can lead individuals to become unwilling to give up their possessions over time, leading to repeated patterns of wealth concentration.

- **Systems, Scale, and Corruption** Fulvio Ciano and Hanif Janmohamed agreed that both individuals and the systems they create can be sources of corruption. Fulvio Ciano posited that the fundamental issue with many systems, whether capitalist or socialist, is the problem of "scale," as systems do not function the same way at different sizes, and the drive for growth exacerbates this. Fulvio Ciano concluded that AI is a major disruptor because it impacts the relationship between labor and resources, a foundational element of historical social systems.

- **The Purpose of Education and Resilience** Ying criticized the current university and school systems for "babysitting" students and failing to prepare them for the challenges of the real world by not systematically training resilience and the ability to face adversity. David Olsson acknowledged that the job of education, at least at the grade school level, has evolved into babysitting, historically rooted in freeing parents to work in industry. David Olsson advocated for applied learning that integrates emotional experience and context-specific thinking, arguing that university often fails to achieve this.

- **Social Skills and Workplace Success** Ying expressed concern that students are not ready for the "brutal" workplace, but David Olsson responded that success is often achieved socially. David Olsson shared that their sons are finding success in business by focusing on social interactions like drinks and golfing, rather than just technical acumen.

- **UBI and the Crisis of Meaning** Hanif Janmohamed highlighted that replacing labor with AI removes a key pillar of capitalism. Ying and Hanif Janmohamed discussed the societal crisis that UBI would create by removing the identity, achievement, and meaning that people attach to work in a production-oriented economy. Jason Kryski suggested that a massive societal reorientation, possibly towards something like space exploration, would be needed to align values with a non-labor-based system.

- **The Challenge of Transition and Localized AI Initiatives** Fulvio Ciano noted that the major obstacle to adopting a new, more sustainable system is the difficulty of the transition and the powerful vested interests that would resist change. Jason Kryski introduced the "Build Canada" initiative, backed by the Shopify founders, as an effort to create localized ecosystems for AI implementation. Jason Kryski is exploring a test bed in Kelowna to arm students with AI and business skills to support the adoption of AI in small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs).

- **Collaboration for AI Enablement in the Community** Ying confirmed the value of Jason Kryski's localized initiative, mentioning that their students have successfully developed functional AI tools for local small and non-profit companies. Ying expressed a desire to plug into the Build Canada initiative to ensure that the local community benefits from AI tools, increasing productivity and competitive advantage. Both agreed that localized efforts are a realistic path forward compared to attempting to restructure the entire society.

- **Integrating AI Skills into Daily Life** Ying discussed the importance of acquiring basic AI skills for everyday use, emphasizing that the goal is not a drastic career change but enabling people to use AI for tasks like calculating the calories in a meal from a photo or scheduling meetings using a screenshot with tools like Chat GPT. Ying believes there is a significant gap in filling this need for widespread AI skill adoption.

- **Socioeconomic Impact of AI and Job Transition** Jason Kryski suggested that while AI can create new opportunities that offset job losses, the challenge lies in the fact that the people losing jobs are often not the ones benefiting from the new AI-related roles. They proposed focusing on how to train those displaced from the economy by AI to leverage these tools to successfully re-enter the workforce in different areas.

- **AI's Potential and Misconceptions** Ying expressed optimism about AI solutions and their functionality, such as multi-language support that can aid in immigrant countries when people need to consult doctors but do not speak perfect English. Ying stated that people who view AI as a threat often do not fully understand its potential to assist with day-to-day tasks, such as creating a tailored gift-buying list based on budget and preferences for Christmas shopping.

- **Tool Recommendation and Group Value** Hanif Janmohamed shared a link for Ying concerning an interesting tool called Kora related to using AI for inbox and email, which Ying expressed interest in. Ying concluded by expressing enthusiasm for the group, noting they learn a lot every time, with Hanif Janmohamed agreeing that these are good things to think about.
