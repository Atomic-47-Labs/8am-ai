## Where the group let itself speculate

Small but indispensable, the future-and-society thread is where 8am AI stops
talking shop and lets itself think about what all of this *means* — for
work, for democracy, for what a person is. It's the corpus's philosophical
edge, and it's the thread most willing to be uncomfortable.

## The arc

- **2024 — structuring experience, and the dark side of data.** It opens
  personally: the shift from structuring *information* to structuring
  *experience* — a career-reflection that doubles as a thesis about where
  value is going. Then it turns sober: Jason on Cambridge Analytica and
  Facebook data in political campaigns — a concrete reminder that this
  technology's social effects are not hypothetical.
- **2025 — roadmaps and sovereignty.** The group documents themes for future
  development, then widens out: the AI Sovereignty conference in Montreal,
  policy discussions, the question of who controls the substrate at a national
  scale. AI as society-shaping infrastructure, not just a tool.
- **Late 2025 — "we've seen this movie."** David's grounding move: AI's
  societal impact is like e-commerce and smartphones — technologies that
  already remade daily life, mostly without anyone voting on it. The framing
  is neither doom nor hype; it's *this is how big technologies actually
  land — gradually, then completely.*
- **2026 — interfaces, and the human after.** Ying argues current interfaces
  are "inside the box," not the future. Then the thread reaches its sharpest
  point: Fulvio and David on the intersection of human and LLM thought-space —
  Neuralink as a worrying proposition, Ozempic as the template for how society
  normalizes a powerful intervention, and the question of what's left of the
  human when the tools get this intimate.

## The two moods

The thread holds two moods without resolving them, which is what makes it
honest:

**Continuity.** The calmest voice in the corpus: we've absorbed
world-changing technologies before. Smartphones rewired attention,
relationships, and politics, and life went on, changed. AI is that, again,
bigger. Adapt.

**Rupture.** The uneasy voice: this one reaches into thought itself.
Neuralink, AI companions, the erosion of the line between your cognition and
the model's. Ozempic shows the pattern — a powerful intervention goes from
taboo to normal in a few years, and people reorganize their lives around it.
What happens when the intervention is *cognitive*?

The group never picks. It holds both — and that refusal to resolve is the
thread's real contribution. The future is probably continuous *and* a
rupture, and pretending it's only one is how you get it wrong.

## What stayed true

1. **The social effects are not hypothetical.** From Cambridge Analytica to
   AI sovereignty, the corpus treats AI's societal impact as a present fact to
   reckon with, not a sci-fi premise.
2. **Big technologies land gradually, then completely.** The smartphone
   template: adaptation feels optional until it isn't.
3. **The intimate frontier is different.** Tools that reach into cognition
   (Neuralink, companions) aren't just bigger versions of past tech — they
   change the thing using them.

## Open question

If the Ozempic pattern holds — taboo to normal in a few years — then the
cognitive interventions the group finds worrying will likely become
unremarkable faster than anyone expects. The corpus sees this coming and
doesn't pretend to know what a person is on the other side of it. That
honesty is the point. The future-and-society thread asks the questions the
rest of the corpus is too busy building to ask.
